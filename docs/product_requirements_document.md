## Project PRD: Establishing a Core Design System

### 1. Executive Summary

This project aims to establish a comprehensive and unified **Design System** to ensure visual consistency, accelerate development, and enhance the overall user experience across our applications. This initiative directly addresses the explicit request to "create the `design system`" and builds upon recent design efforts for projects such as the Intelligence CRM. By formalizing our design language, we will establish a robust and scalable foundation that improves efficiency and reduces potential for future inconsistencies.

---

### 2. Background & Motivation

The recent "Deep Design Session Complete" for the Intelligence CRM (PRD ID: `eb4d921efdfd4fba805bdc546761217`) and its generated screen (Intelligence Feed: `9f2bdc23fc7b4faea1187c27e5f56c30`) highlighted the critical importance of "visual consistency across the application." To formalize and scale this consistency, and to prevent potential future design inconsistencies or rework (addressing the implied "retry the failed one" sentiment by ensuring a robust solution going forward), a dedicated Design System is essential. It will serve as a single source of truth for design principles, guidelines, and UI components, promoting efficiency and cohesion across our product suite.

---

### 3. Goals

*   **Primary Goal:** Create a foundational Design System that encompasses core UI elements, design tokens, and comprehensive usage guidelines.
*   **Secondary Goals:**
    *   Ensure consistent branding and user experience across all product touchpoints, with an initial focus on integrating with the Intelligence CRM.
    *   Streamline design and development workflows, improving efficiency and reducing time-to-market for new features and iterations.
    *   Establish a shared language and single source of truth between design, product, and engineering teams.
    *   Improve the maintainability and scalability of our codebase and design assets.
    *   Empower faster prototyping and iteration with pre-built, accessible components.

---

### 4. Scope & Deliverables

The Design System will encompass the following key areas and produce the specified deliverables:

**In Scope:**

*   **Design Principles:** Clearly articulated principles guiding design decisions and ensuring brand alignment.
*   **Design Tokens:** A comprehensive set of definable values for foundational stylistic attributes (e.g., colors, typography, spacing, shadows, border-radii). These will be implemented in a format consumable by both design tools and development environments.
*   **UI Component Library:** A curated collection of reusable UI components (e.g., buttons, inputs, cards, navigation elements, dialogs) with defined properties, states, behaviors, and accessibility considerations.
*   **Documentation Portal:** A central repository detailing how to use, contribute to, and maintain the Design System, including usage guidelines, code examples, and accessibility best practices.
*   **Design Assets:** Figma/Sketch/XD libraries containing the Design Tokens and UI Component Library.
*   **Initial Integration Guidance:** Support, documentation, and potentially starter code for integrating the Design System into the Intelligence CRM project (`eb4d921efdfd4fba805bdc546761217`) as its primary initial consumer.

**Out of Scope:**

*   Full brand identity guidelines (e.g., logo creation, extensive marketing collateral, brand voice beyond UI context).
*   Specific feature development or product enhancements within Intelligence CRM or other products, beyond the consumption and application of Design System components.
*   Development of every conceivable component in the first iteration (focus will be on core, most-used components).

### 6. Success Metrics
